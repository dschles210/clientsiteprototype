Biblical References Throughout this Novel



Creation/Garden of Eden

“Once we lived in a summer country. In the woods there were treehouses, and on the lake there were boats” (Millet 1).

“‘You know how, in that book the lady gave me, after they left that beautiful garden they got in a really big flood’” (Millet 70).

The Fall: “That was how we could tell it was serious. Because they were obviously lying. We knew who was responsible, of course: it had been a done deal before we were born” (Millet 27)

“‘Do you blame us?’ asked a mother. Pathetic-sounding. ‘We blame you for everything,’ said Jen evenly. ‘Who else is there to blame?’ added Rafe” (Millet 193).


The Flood/Noah's Ark

“‘Evie,' said Jack. ‘We have to save the animals. Like Noah did’” (Millet 70).

“Later we slept hunched together on a nearby platform-certainly smaller than the ark, but decently covered” (Millet 73).








Reincarnation of Christ

“When the top of the baby’s head was emerging and angels were murmuring words of encouragement… the baby wailed. That was how Sukey got a sister” (Millet 112).











Death and Resurrection of Christ

“He was sick by then, but I was going to make sure he got better. Whenever I wasn’t by his bedside, I was researching symptoms and diagnoses” (Millet 224).

When Jack is tired and in bed, sick, Eve says that he “solved” that Jesus was science and art was the Holy Ghost that was all the things that people make. Eve says, “And I went on. The clouds, the moon. The dirt, the rocks, the water and the wind. We call that hope you see” (Millet 224).
